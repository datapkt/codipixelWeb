Aerial Videography

Drone videography (or aerial videography) captures images and video by a remotely operated aircraft. It has become one of the most popular methods of obtaining a location or product video that would otherwise be next to impossible to achieve. People often use unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) to film vast spaces of land, such as construction sites, landscapes, windmills, etc. However, one can also use these drones to fly near sensitive areas and places in ways standard cameras and their operators canâ€™t.

The essence of Aerial Videography is captured through our top-notch professional drones paired with versatile high-resolution sensors and cameras. Still, a premium drone is nothing without a certified and experienced drone operator. Our established drone operators undoubtedly deliver great aerial photographs and video clips as they embrace the expertise and creativity required. Highways and Roads

  • Aerial videography of roads using the unmanned aircrafts can solve land acquisition and liaison issues by providing insights on the acquired and unacquired land perimeters. It can help in clearing out the bottlenecks and keep the project on schedule by providing precise and concise information upto date.
  • Early access to information like public hospitals, vegetation, religious structures, etc. the respective project managers can make well informed decisions on whether to shift those utilities or demolish them.
  • aerial survey data is integrated with geographical coordinates and corresponding chainage, providing accurate location of important structures. This improves the efficiency of communication between people working onsite as well as offsite.
  • Quarterly aerial videography of the entire stretch can provide timely updates on the progress. Any bottlenecks that hamper the progress can immediately be solved once the project manager has the data in hand to rectify.

Railways and Metro Rail Services

  • Aerial inspection using quadcopters can aid the railways and agencies to keep track of their critical assets and avoid catastrophic damage to OHEs (OverHead Equipment)
  • Drones can collect aerial data quickly while assuring the appropriate safety standards even on busy days and excessive traffic on the metro tracks without interfering with their train schedule.
  • Drone surveillance has emerged as an important and cost effective tool for security surveillance in many areas with minimal manpower.
  • It is very useful while mapping railway assets to assess the encroachments on railway property.
  • UAVs have evolved to fill gaps of conventional railway inspection systems and developed the operation with fewer interruptions, more effectiveness, and efficiency.

Mining

  • Drones offer real-time monitoring and inspection of deep shafts, difficult to access manually. Also, when surveying mines with drones, engineers can monitor the performance off-site as well.
  • Drones can accurately click pictures and check road widths and windrow heights to ensure the efficiency and safety standards are met. The aerial images are more precise and reliable than any other traditional monitoring and inspection methods, not to forget, quicker too.
  • With high-quality drones and a certified drone operator on site, the cost for detailed aerial photography is significantly lesser than that of a traditional pilot flying a manned aircraft.
  • The mining survey helps to obtain aerial data of mining sites for better planning, productivity, and inventory management. The ground software helps to organize the same.
  • By deploying UAVs to capture aerial data, regular visual evaluations of haul roads can take place. Obtaining relevant information such as length, slopes, and turning angles of the haul roads support engineers and constructors for meticulous preparation, designing, development and upkeep.

Thermal

  • Comprehensive thermal videography using unmanned aircrafts help identify damages or performance anomalies on cooling tower structures, inlets, and outlets. A visual representation of temperatures in each plenum makes it easy to spot anomalies across the tower and rectify before further damage.
  • A boiler inspection using traditional methods is a long and expensive process, taking about eight days once the boiler has cooled off. It takes two days to install the scaffolding, up to four days to inspect, and another two days to remove the scaffolding. Drones cut the inspection time from a few days to a couple of hours. No scaffolding is required, hazardous man-hours are reduced, and inspections are completed at a fraction of the cost.
  • Large structures are particularly well suited for drone inspections. A drone can conduct a visual inspection in a fraction of the time as well as cost, as compared to other methods, while providing data that is more comprehensive and detailed than being done manually.
  • Drones capture high-resolution images from all sides of joints and hangers to identify cracks or other damages and load indicators. Detailed, visual inspection data can be captured using entry-level commercial drones and even basic drone operating skills. These are quality inspections that are easily attainable and eliminate climbing or costly scaffold construction.
  • Thermal plants register some of the highest wear and tear of industrial assets. Aerial videography, thermal plant mapping, asset tagging, sample collection, safety and surveillance keep a check on all of the mentioned.

Solar Plants

  • Through drone surveys and thermal mapping of solar power plants, essential information can be recovered. Precise solar farm inspection can spot cell defects, cell failure, dirt on photovoltaic cells, interconnection failure, degradation of solar cells, decrease of operational efficiency, etc. Humans cannot implement thermal mapping and aerial infrared imaging alone, within the stipulated time.
  • A large hexacopter fly-by with the appropriate hardware as its payload during a construction can provide progress reporting, project milestone completion validation, quality control, and logistics planning for easier project management and stakeholder oversight.
  • Drones offer an efficient and effective method of understanding the quality and operational health of solar farms. In some scenarios, asset purchasers, financiers, or energy off-takers have a limited amount of time to assess and accept a new solar farm.
  • Drones can be used to create measurable images of roofs and to identify possible obstructions for pre-installation planning.
  • Drones can provide detailed imagery of transmission lines and poles, allowing workers to identify defects without climbing or using bucket trucks.

Oil and Gas

  • Drones are being equipped with high-definition video and infrared thermal imaging sensors, laser leak detection systems and high-resolution cameras which record images as well as motion.
  • Drones can help oil and gas companies to carry out aerial, underground and undersea explorations with the appropriate machine.
  • The data from oil rigs, offshore platforms and other areas provide valuable information that companies can use to improve operations, production and efficiency as well as ability to forecast and address breakdowns.
  • Drone inspections enable researchers to collect vital information from prospective oil wells. Using other technologies such as laser scanning and GPS, the drones will provide topographical information about the new locations even before dispatching people to the ground.
  • The drones provide a faster and cost effective means of inspecting pipelines - something which would traditionally take weeks or months with expensive equipment, resources and manpower.

Manufacturing plant

  • Drones have significant potential for manufacturers with the ability to transform the production process.
  • They help lower the operational and management costs to a minimum, timely and accurate maintenance and vastly reduced operational risk.
  • Drones can be helpful in quickly scanning the warehouses and inventory.
  • For people owning multiple manufacturing plants, far from each other, videography based drones, one at each plant can help record visuals and save time.
  • Drone videography can be used for perimeter surveillance or surveillance of specific plant areas and for safety audit purposes.

Forest

  • Aerial videography can be used in forest mapping and this has already profited countries like Indonesia. A small UAV was flown over a forested area and this was able to return data that showed the vastness of the forest and other relevant information including but not limited to human activities around the forest.
  • Different parameters relating to forests such as the extent of canopy cover, the number of trees and volume estimation can also be achieved efficiently through the use of unmanned aircrafts. This data collected by drones is more accurate than any other method and can help in quick and better decision making as opposed to other methods.
  • Forest disturbances are mostly caused by wind and snow and these have a direct impact on the productivity of forest stands. This also affects regeneration. Satellite imagery was initially used to access this kind of data by mapping the canopy gaps but the information was not as accurate as it needed to be. This however changed with the introduction of drones, who record visuals from a much closer altitude than the space satellite.
  • Drones are now being used for aerial surveys of rainforests to determine where the trees have been affected by human activities and the patterns being used to achieve this. Drones can then relay the data to the relevant authorities who use it to make informed decisions.
  • Drone videography can be used to monitor illegal quarrying especially in forested areas that are out of reach by law enforcement personnel. Drones can fly over these areas and take clear photographs of the damage that has been caused in these areas and advise authorities on corrective measures to take.

Air Pollution

  • Drone videography allows for a comprehensive inspection of the surrounding elements which cause air pollution. It allows for monitoring round the clock.
  • Spaces hitherto inaccessible due to tough terrain or climatic conditions can also be easily monitored with aerial videography.
  • Along with UAVs equipped with sensors, drones can calculate the pollution level and do live-reporting along with accurate images and videos of the location.
  • The adverse effects of the air pollution can also be checked using drone videography for insurance purposes. This helps as a value addition during policy-making.

Bathymetry

  • Drone videography can be used for river and lake bottom profiling for scientific investigations and environmental monitoring, underwater inspections for engineering works such as bridge or pipeline crossings, and for sludge volume measurements at waste stabilization ponds.
  • Drones are also used in bathymetric surveys of ponds in open pits, mapping of sediments of oxidation and tailing ponds, and mapping water depths and sediments before dredging and cleaning of ponds, lakes, rivers, and canals.
  • It is better to use a drone with an echo sounder for mapping, measuring and inspecting tasks and environmental monitoring if one is conducting bathymetric surveys of tailings dams and ponds.

Drones have reduced cost for aerial footage compared to manned helicopters. It has made it possible to record in small or confined spaces full scale helicopters cannot access. It can capture new perspectives outside the usual vision from the ground. It has become one of the most popular methods of obtaining a location or product video that would typically be impossible to achieve.
